Global warming stands as one of the greatest challenges confronting modern civilization. The principal driver of rising temperatures worldwide is the release of greenhouse gases including carbon dioxide and methane into the atmosphere. These emissions trap solar heat, producing what scientists call the greenhouse effect.

Researchers have documented a notable uptick in catastrophic weather phenomena over recent decades. More frequent and intense storms, prolonged droughts, and severe flooding have been observed globally. The melting of Arctic and Antarctic ice is causing ocean levels to rise, endangering low-lying coastal populations.

Solar energy and wind turbines represent key technologies to cut carbon emissions and address climate challenges. Both public and private sectors need to commit substantial resources to developing clean energy systems as a replacement for coal and oil. Consumers can also contribute by lowering their energy footprint and opting for environmentally responsible goods.

Global treaties and multilateral agreements are indispensable for a coordinated response to climate change. The Paris Climate Accord was a historic step by world governments to keep temperature increases under 2 degrees Celsius above pre-industrial levels. Nonetheless, the commitments made so far fall short of what science demands, and stronger measures are necessary without delay.

Raising public knowledge and fostering environmental literacy are fundamental to solving the climate crisis. Informed citizens tend to advocate for stronger environmental regulations and alter their consumption habits for the better. Student-led climate movements globally have shown how organized advocacy can influence institutional and governmental responses.
