Climate change is one of the most pressing issues facing humanity today. The rise in global temperatures is primarily caused by the emission of greenhouse gases such as carbon dioxide and methane. These gases trap heat in the Earth's atmosphere, leading to a gradual warming effect known as the greenhouse effect.

Scientists have observed a significant increase in extreme weather events over the past few decades. Hurricanes, droughts, and floods have become more frequent and severe. Sea levels are rising as polar ice caps melt, threatening coastal communities around the world.

Renewable energy sources such as solar and wind power offer a promising solution to reduce carbon emissions. Governments and corporations must invest heavily in clean energy infrastructure to transition away from fossil fuels. Individual actions, such as reducing energy consumption and choosing sustainable products, also play an important role.

International cooperation is essential to address climate change effectively. The Paris Agreement represents a landmark commitment by nations to limit global warming to well below 2 degrees Celsius. However, current pledges are insufficient to meet this target, and more ambitious action is urgently needed.

Education and public awareness are critical components of the climate solution. When people understand the science and the stakes, they are more likely to support policies and make lifestyle changes that benefit the environment. Youth movements around the world have demonstrated the power of grassroots activism in driving climate action.
